## My Core Belief

**Purpose**: State your belief boldly and clearly so there is no ambiguity
**Target length**: ~200 words

Open with "I believe..." and state your conviction in one clear sentence. Then expand on what this belief means in practice — how does it shape your decisions, your work, and your interactions? Explain when you first came to hold this belief and what solidified it over time. Do not hedge or qualify excessively — this is a piece about conviction. However, distinguish between confidence and arrogance: state your belief with strength while acknowledging it as your perspective, not universal truth. The reader should know exactly where you stand by the end of this section.

## Why I Believe This

**Purpose**: Build the case for your belief with evidence and experience
**Target length**: ~250 words

Present 2-3 pieces of supporting evidence. Mix personal experience with external validation — your own story matters, but it is strengthened by research, data, expert agreement, or historical precedent. For each piece of evidence:

- Describe the experience or cite the source.
- Explain how it reinforced or shaped your belief.
- Connect it to a principle or pattern that the reader can verify in their own life.

Be genuine rather than exhaustive. The goal is not to prove your belief beyond doubt but to show the reader that it is grounded in real experience and thoughtful consideration.

## The Other Side

**Purpose**: Address the strongest counterargument with intellectual honesty
**Target length**: ~200 words

Steel-man the opposing view. Present the strongest version of the counterargument — not a straw man, but the version that a thoughtful, well-meaning person would actually hold. Acknowledge what is valid about it. Then explain why, despite understanding this perspective, you still hold your belief. This might involve identifying where the counterargument breaks down, where it applies but does not generalize, or where the trade-offs favor your position. This section builds enormous credibility because it shows you have genuinely engaged with the other side.

## Join Me

**Purpose**: Invite the reader to consider adopting or exploring this belief
**Target length**: ~150 words

Shift from argumentation to invitation. You are not trying to win a debate — you are extending a hand. Ask the reader to consider what would change in their life or work if they adopted this belief. Suggest a small experiment or thought exercise they could try. Acknowledge that changing beliefs takes time and courage. Close with a statement of solidarity: you are not alone in this belief, and there is a community of people who share it. Invite engagement — comments, replies, conversations — to continue the discussion.
