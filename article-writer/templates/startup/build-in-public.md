## The Project

**Purpose**: Introduce or re-introduce the project and what you are building
**Target length**: ~150 words

For new readers, briefly explain what you are building and why. For returning readers, provide a one-sentence recap and then focus on what has changed since the last update. State the project's mission or goal in clear, simple terms. Include any key metrics or milestones that define success. If the project has pivoted or evolved since you last wrote, explain what changed and why. Keep this section concise — regular followers want to get to the updates quickly.

## Current Status

**Purpose**: Give a transparent snapshot of where things stand
**Target length**: ~150 words

Report on progress honestly. What got done since the last update? What did not get done and why? Share specific numbers where possible: users, revenue, features shipped, bugs fixed, hours invested. If progress is slower than expected, say so without over-explaining. If something went wrong, describe it directly. Build-in-public audiences value honesty over polish. Include a simple progress indicator — percentage complete, milestone checklist, or phase indicator — so readers can track the journey over time.

## What I'm Learning

**Purpose**: Share the real-time lessons that are shaping your approach
**Target length**: ~150 words

This is the most valuable section for your audience. Share 2-3 lessons from this phase of the build. These might be technical discoveries, market insights, personal realizations, or process improvements. Be specific: "I learned that X" is better than "I'm learning a lot." Include at least one lesson that surprised you or contradicted your expectations. Readers follow build-in-public journeys for the unfiltered learning, not the polished success story.

## Next Steps

**Purpose**: Set expectations for the next phase and invite accountability
**Target length**: ~150 words

List 3-5 specific next steps with rough timelines. Be realistic — over-promising and under-delivering erodes trust in a build-in-public context. Identify the biggest risk or uncertainty for the next phase. Ask for input where genuinely helpful — recommendations, introductions, beta testers, or feedback. Close with a date or trigger for the next update so readers know when to check back. Thank your audience for following along and remind them how to stay connected.
