## Headline

**Purpose**: Capture attention and communicate the core value proposition in seconds
**Target length**: ~50 words

Write a headline that is clear, benefit-driven, and specific. Avoid cleverness at the expense of clarity. The headline should answer: "What is this, and why should I care?" Follow the headline with a subheadline (1-2 sentences) that expands on the promise and addresses the target customer directly. Consider using a power word (free, proven, instant, effortless) if authentic. Test multiple headline variations — the headline is the single highest-leverage element on the page.

## Benefits

**Purpose**: Show the reader how their life or work improves with this product
**Target length**: ~300 words

Present 3-5 key benefits, each with a clear heading. Focus on outcomes, not features — "Save 10 hours per week" rather than "Automated scheduling." For each benefit:

- State the benefit in outcome-oriented language.
- Provide one sentence of supporting detail explaining how it works.
- Address the underlying pain point the benefit resolves.

Order benefits from most compelling to least. Use parallel structure for consistency. Include visual cues (icons, short paragraphs, bullet points) that make the section scannable. Every benefit should pass the "so what?" test from the customer's perspective.

## Social Proof

**Purpose**: Build trust and reduce perceived risk through evidence from others
**Target length**: ~250 words

Include 2-3 customer testimonials with specific results, not generic praise. "We increased conversions by 34% in two months" is far more persuasive than "Great product!" Each testimonial should include the person's name, title, and company for credibility.

Supplement testimonials with quantitative social proof: number of customers, companies served, industry awards, media mentions, or aggregate statistics ("Trusted by 10,000+ teams"). If you have logos of recognizable clients, reference their placement here. Address common objections indirectly through testimonials — choose quotes that counter the reader's likely hesitations.

## Call to Action

**Purpose**: Drive the reader to take one specific, clear action
**Target length**: ~200 words

Restate the core value proposition in one sentence. Address the reader's final hesitation — offer a guarantee, free trial, or risk-reversal that lowers the barrier. Make the CTA button text action-oriented and specific ("Start My Free Trial" beats "Submit"). Include a brief note about what happens after clicking — people want to know what to expect. Add urgency if authentic (limited time offer, limited spots). Close with a secondary CTA for readers not ready to commit (newsletter signup, free resource, demo request).
