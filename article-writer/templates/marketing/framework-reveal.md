## The Problem

**Purpose**: Establish the pain point that motivates the need for a framework
**Target length**: ~200 words

Describe the problem in vivid, relatable terms. Show that you understand the reader's frustration deeply — use language they would use themselves. Explain why existing approaches fall short: they are too complex, too generic, or too slow. Quantify the cost of the problem if possible. Build tension that makes the reader eager for a solution. End with a transition that hints at a better way, setting up the framework reveal.

## The Framework

**Purpose**: Introduce and explain each step of the framework in detail
**Target length**: ~400 words (approximately 100 words per step)

Introduce the framework by name and give a one-sentence overview of what it does. Then break it into 3-4 clear steps:

### Step 1

Name the step with a memorable, action-oriented label. Explain what this step involves in practical terms. Describe the specific actions the reader should take and the outcome they should expect. Include a brief tip or common pitfall to avoid.

### Step 2

Build on Step 1 with the next logical phase. Explain the transition from the previous step. Detail the actions, tools, or mindset required. Show how this step compounds the progress from Step 1.

### Step 3

Continue the progression. By this point, the reader should feel momentum building. Make the connection between steps explicit so the framework feels cohesive, not like a random list.

### Step 4

Deliver the culminating step that brings everything together. Explain the final outcome and how to know the framework has been applied successfully. Connect back to the problem statement to show the full arc from pain to resolution.

## Example: How I Used It

**Purpose**: Prove the framework works through a real-world application
**Target length**: ~300 words

Walk through a concrete example of the framework in action. Use a real project, client, or personal experience. For each step, show what you specifically did and what happened as a result. Include measurable outcomes where possible. Be honest about any challenges or adjustments you made along the way — this adds credibility. The example should make the framework feel achievable and practical, not theoretical.

## Try It Yourself

**Purpose**: Invite the reader to apply the framework and engage further
**Target length**: ~200 words

Encourage the reader to try the framework on their own challenge. Suggest a specific, low-stakes starting point. Provide a simple worksheet, checklist, or prompt they can use immediately. Offer additional resources — a deeper guide, a workshop, a community — for readers who want to go further. Include a CTA: download, subscribe, book a call, or simply reply with their results. Close with confidence that the framework will deliver results.
