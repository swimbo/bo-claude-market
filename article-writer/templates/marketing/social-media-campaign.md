## Strategy

**Purpose**: Define the campaign's goals, audience, and overarching approach
**Target length**: ~100 words

State the campaign goal in one sentence with a measurable target. Identify the target audience and what motivates them. Define the campaign's tone and visual identity in 2-3 adjectives. Outline the campaign timeline (launch, peak, wrap-up) and posting cadence. Specify the key performance indicators that will measure success. Every piece of content that follows should ladder up to this strategy.

## Platform Content

**Purpose**: Create tailored content for each social media platform
**Target length**: ~300 words

### Twitter/X

Write 3-4 tweet variations (max 280 characters each). Lead with the most provocative or curiosity-driven version. Include a mix of formats: a question, a bold statement, a data point, and a thread opener. Optimize for retweets by making tweets self-contained and shareable. Include relevant hashtags naturally within the text.

### Instagram

Write a caption (125-150 words) for the primary campaign post. Open with a hook in the first line (it appears before the "more" fold). Use line breaks for readability. End with a clear CTA (comment, save, share, link in bio). Include 5-10 relevant hashtags grouped at the end. Describe the ideal image or carousel concept in brackets.

### LinkedIn

Write a professional post (150-200 words) adapted for LinkedIn's audience. Open with a hook that triggers curiosity or recognition. Use short paragraphs and line breaks. Share an insight or data point relevant to the professional audience. End with a question to drive comments and engagement. Keep the tone authoritative but approachable.

## Hashtags

**Purpose**: Define the hashtag strategy for reach and tracking
**Target length**: ~100 words

Create a branded campaign hashtag that is unique, memorable, and easy to spell. Pair it with 5-8 topical hashtags that your target audience already follows. Research hashtag volume — aim for a mix of high-volume (broad reach) and niche (targeted reach) tags. Avoid hashtags with ambiguous meanings or those co-opted by unrelated communities. Specify which hashtags to use on which platforms, as hashtag culture varies significantly across Twitter/X, Instagram, and LinkedIn.
