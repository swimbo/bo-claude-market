## Lead

**Purpose**: Deliver the most important facts in the opening paragraph
**Target length**: ~100 words

Write a lead paragraph that answers the five W's (who, what, when, where, why) and how in the most concise way possible. The lead should be one paragraph, ideally 1-3 sentences. Lead with the most newsworthy element — what makes this story matter right now? Use active voice and strong verbs. Include specific names, numbers, and dates rather than vague references. A reader who only reads the lead should walk away understanding the core story.

## Supporting Details

**Purpose**: Expand on the lead with additional context and facts
**Target length**: ~300 words

Build out the story in order of decreasing importance (inverted pyramid). The first paragraphs after the lead should provide the most critical supporting details: How did this happen? What are the immediate consequences? Who is directly affected?

Follow with broader context: Is this part of a trend? How does it compare to similar events? What is the historical backdrop? Include relevant data, timelines, or technical details that help the reader understand the full picture. Each paragraph should be able to be cut from the bottom without losing the core story — this is the discipline of news writing.

## Quotes

**Purpose**: Add human voices and expert perspectives to the story
**Target length**: ~200 words

Include 2-4 direct quotes from relevant sources. Each quote should add something that factual reporting alone cannot — emotion, authority, controversy, or insider perspective. Attribute every quote clearly with the speaker's full name, title, and organization on first reference.

Alternate between quotes and paraphrased context. Avoid long block quotes — select the most powerful 1-2 sentences. Include perspectives from multiple sides if the story involves disagreement or debate. A quote should never simply restate what the reporter has already written in their own words.

## Background

**Purpose**: Provide the historical or contextual backdrop that gives the story depth
**Target length**: ~200 words

Place the story in its broader context. How did we get here? What previous events or decisions led to this moment? Include relevant history, policy background, or industry context that helps the reader understand why this news matters beyond today. Reference previous reporting or coverage if applicable. End with a forward-looking element: What happens next? What are the unresolved questions? What should the reader watch for?
