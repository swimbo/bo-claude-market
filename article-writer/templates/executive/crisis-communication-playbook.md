## Situation Assessment

**Purpose**: Document the facts of the crisis clearly and objectively
**Target length**: ~200 words

State the nature of the crisis in factual terms. Document what is known, what is unknown, and what is being investigated. Include a timeline of events leading to the crisis. Assess the severity level and the criteria used to determine it. Identify all affected stakeholders: employees, customers, investors, partners, regulators, and the public. Quantify the impact where possible: number of affected users, financial exposure, operational disruption. Note the source of the information and its reliability. This assessment should be updated as new information becomes available and serve as the single source of truth.

## Immediate Actions Taken

**Purpose**: Document the response already underway to demonstrate decisive leadership
**Target length**: ~200 words

List every action taken since the crisis was identified, in chronological order. For each action:

- What was done.
- Who authorized and executed it.
- When it was taken.
- What result or outcome is expected.

Include both operational responses (containing the damage, deploying fixes, engaging experts) and communication responses (internal notifications, external statements, regulatory filings). If a crisis team has been assembled, list the members and their roles. Document any decisions deferred and the rationale for waiting. This section creates an audit trail and demonstrates that leadership is responding with urgency and competence.

## Key Messages Framework

**Purpose**: Establish the consistent core messages to be used across all communications
**Target length**: ~200 words

Define 3-5 key messages that will anchor all crisis communications. Each message should:

- Be clear, honest, and empathetic.
- Acknowledge the situation without speculating beyond known facts.
- Demonstrate accountability and commitment to resolution.
- Include a forward-looking element about remediation.

Establish message discipline: everyone communicating about the crisis should use these messages consistently. Define what should not be said — topics to avoid, speculation to prevent, and legal boundaries. Include a holding statement for questions that cannot yet be answered: "We are actively investigating and will share updates as we confirm additional details." Specify who approves communications before release.

## Internal Communication

**Purpose**: Communicate with employees transparently and equip them to respond
**Target length**: ~200 words

Draft the internal communication to employees. Employees are your first audience — they need to hear from leadership before reading about it externally. Include:

- What happened, stated honestly.
- What the company is doing about it.
- What employees should and should not do (especially regarding external inquiries).
- Where to direct questions.
- When the next update will come.

Equip customer-facing employees with talking points and an escalation path. Address employee concerns directly: Is their data affected? Are their jobs impacted? Will the company recover? The internal communication should feel like leadership trusting its team with the truth and asking for their partnership in navigating the crisis.

## Customer Communication

**Purpose**: Address customers directly with transparency, empathy, and actionable information
**Target length**: ~200 words

Draft the customer-facing communication. Lead with empathy: acknowledge the impact on them before discussing what happened. Include:

- A clear description of what happened and how it affects them specifically.
- What the company is doing to fix it and prevent recurrence.
- What the customer needs to do, if anything (change passwords, contact support, etc.).
- How to reach support for individual concerns.
- A timeline for resolution or next update.

The tone should be human, not corporate. Use "we" and "you" language. Apologize if appropriate — genuine apologies build trust. Avoid legalistic language that prioritizes liability protection over customer care. If compensation or remediation is offered, state it clearly.

## Investor Communication

**Purpose**: Provide investors with a factual assessment and confidence in leadership's response
**Target length**: ~200 words

Draft the investor communication. Investors need facts, financial impact assessment, and confidence that leadership has the situation under control. Include:

- A factual summary of the crisis.
- Known and estimated financial impact.
- Actions taken and the response plan.
- Risk assessment and mitigation strategy.
- Timeline for resolution and follow-up communication.

The tone should be measured, transparent, and confident. Acknowledge uncertainty honestly — investors distrust leaders who minimize problems. Demonstrate that the board has been informed and is engaged. If the crisis has material financial implications, coordinate with legal and finance teams on disclosure requirements. Specify when the next update will be provided.

## Media Communication

**Purpose**: Control the external narrative with proactive, factual media engagement
**Target length**: ~200 words

Draft the press statement or media response. Identify the designated spokesperson and their preparation needs. Include:

- The official statement: concise, factual, and empathetic.
- Approved talking points for the spokesperson.
- Anticipated media questions and prepared responses.
- Topics that are off-limits and bridge statements to redirect.
- The media contact point for all inquiries.

Decide on the communication strategy: proactive disclosure or reactive response? In most cases, proactive disclosure with a strong narrative is preferable to reacting to leaked information. Prepare the spokesperson with mock Q&A. Define the media monitoring process to track coverage and respond to inaccuracies quickly.

## Crisis Response Checklist

**Purpose**: Provide a comprehensive operational checklist for managing the crisis
**Target length**: ~200 words

Create a prioritized checklist organized by timeframe:

**Within 1 hour:**
- Assemble crisis team.
- Confirm facts and assess severity.
- Issue internal notification.
- Activate communication protocol.

**Within 4 hours:**
- Issue customer communication.
- Prepare media statement.
- Brief the board and investors.
- Establish monitoring cadence.

**Within 24 hours:**
- Complete root cause analysis (if possible).
- Issue detailed stakeholder updates.
- Begin remediation actions.
- Document all decisions and actions taken.

**Ongoing:**
- Daily situation updates to all stakeholders.
- Monitor media and social channels.
- Update response plan as new information emerges.
- Plan post-crisis review and improvement process.

Include decision escalation criteria: what developments require immediate executive notification? Identify the trigger for standing down from crisis mode.
