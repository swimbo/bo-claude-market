## Decision Context

**Purpose**: Provide the background necessary to understand why this decision is needed
**Target length**: ~150 words

State the situation that necessitates a decision. What has changed, what is at risk, or what opportunity has emerged? Provide enough background that someone unfamiliar with the history can understand the context without being overwhelmed. Reference any previous decisions, data, or events that led to this point. Identify the deadline or urgency driver — why must this be decided now? State the decision question clearly: "The decision before us is whether to..." This section should make the reader understand the decision is necessary and consequential.

## Options Analysis

**Purpose**: Present the alternatives that were considered with honest assessment
**Target length**: ~300 words

Present 2-4 options that were seriously considered. For each option:

- **Description**: What this option entails in practical terms.
- **Pros**: The strongest arguments in favor.
- **Cons**: The honest downsides and risks.
- **Financial impact**: Cost, revenue, or ROI implications.
- **Timeline**: How long implementation would take.
- **Reversibility**: How easily the decision could be unwound if it does not work.

Present options fairly — do not set up a straw man to make the preferred option look better. If an option was suggested by a specific stakeholder, note that. Include the "do nothing" option if applicable, with an honest assessment of the cost of inaction. Use a comparison table for complex multi-criteria decisions.

## Decision & Rationale

**Purpose**: State the decision clearly and explain the reasoning behind it
**Target length**: ~200 words

State the decision in one clear sentence. Then explain the rationale:

- Which factors weighed most heavily in the decision?
- What trade-offs were accepted and why?
- Which risks were deemed acceptable?
- What assumptions underlie the decision?
- Who was consulted and what input influenced the outcome?

Document any dissenting views and how they were addressed. If the decision was not unanimous, note the split and the reasons. This documentation creates an audit trail and prevents revisionism. Specify the conditions under which this decision should be revisited — "If X changes, we should reconsider this decision."

## Implementation Plan

**Purpose**: Outline the steps needed to execute the decision
**Target length**: ~150 words

Break implementation into clear phases with milestones:

- **Immediate actions** (within 1 week): What needs to happen right away.
- **Short-term** (1-4 weeks): Key implementation steps and dependencies.
- **Medium-term** (1-3 months): Full rollout and stabilization.

For each phase, identify the owner, key deliverables, and resources required. Specify any approvals needed before proceeding to the next phase. Identify the critical path — which steps are sequential and which can be parallelized? Include a communication plan for announcing and explaining the decision to affected parties.

## Governance & Accountability

**Purpose**: Establish who owns the outcome and how success will be tracked
**Target length**: ~100 words

Name the implementation owner and their authority level. Define the success criteria — specific, measurable outcomes that will determine whether the decision was sound. Set the review date and what will be evaluated at that point. Specify the escalation path if implementation encounters significant obstacles. Define the decision rights for any subsequent choices that arise during implementation. Include the distribution list for this memo so there is a record of who was informed.

## Communication Plan

**Purpose**: Define how and when the decision will be communicated to stakeholders
**Target length**: ~100 words

Outline the communication sequence: who hears first, through what channel, and what level of detail they receive. Tailor the message for each audience — the board, leadership team, affected employees, customers, and partners may need different framing. Identify the spokesperson for each audience. Prepare for questions and concerns that each group is likely to raise. Specify the timing — communications should be coordinated to prevent information leaking unevenly across the organization.
