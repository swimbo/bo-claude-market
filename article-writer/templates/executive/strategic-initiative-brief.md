## Strategic Alignment

**Purpose**: Connect the initiative to the company's broader strategic objectives
**Target length**: ~100 words

State which strategic objective or company priority this initiative supports. Explain why now is the right time to pursue it — what market conditions, internal capabilities, or competitive pressures make this timely? If this initiative was identified through a planning process, reference it. If it is emerging from an urgent need, explain the trigger. The reader should immediately understand how this initiative fits into the bigger picture and why it deserves attention and resources.

## Business Case

**Purpose**: Present the problem, the proposed solution, and the expected return
**Target length**: ~150 words

Describe the business problem in concrete terms with quantified impact. Then present the proposed solution in 2-3 sentences — what will be built, changed, or implemented? Explain the expected return: revenue increase, cost reduction, risk mitigation, or capability gained. Compare the cost of the initiative to the cost of inaction. If there are alternative approaches that were considered, note why this solution was selected. Keep the business case tight and evidence-based — this section sells the initiative.

## Success Metrics

**Purpose**: Define how success will be measured with specific, measurable criteria
**Target length**: ~100 words

List 3-5 specific metrics with targets and measurement timeframes. Each metric should be:

- **Specific**: What exactly is being measured.
- **Measurable**: The numeric target or threshold.
- **Time-bound**: When the metric will be evaluated.

Include both leading indicators (early signals of progress) and lagging indicators (ultimate outcomes). Identify any baseline measurements needed before the initiative begins. Specify who owns each metric and how it will be tracked.

## Investment & Timeline

**Purpose**: Specify the resources required and the implementation schedule
**Target length**: ~100 words

State the total investment required: budget, headcount, technology, and any external resources. Break the timeline into 3-4 phases with key milestones and deliverables for each. Identify critical dependencies — what must be in place for each phase to begin? Note any existing resources that can be leveraged to reduce cost or accelerate timelines. If the initiative can be staged with go/no-go decision points, highlight where those checkpoints fall.

## Stakeholder Impact

**Purpose**: Identify who is affected and how to manage alignment
**Target length**: ~100 words

Map the key stakeholders: sponsor, owner, contributing teams, and affected parties. For each stakeholder group, note their role in the initiative, their primary concern, and what they need to be successful. Identify any potential resistance and how it will be addressed. Specify the governance structure — who makes decisions, who provides input, and how often the initiative is reviewed. Clear stakeholder alignment prevents the most common cause of initiative failure.

## Risk Considerations

**Purpose**: Acknowledge the top risks concisely
**Target length**: ~50 words

List the 2-3 most significant risks to the initiative's success. For each, provide a one-sentence mitigation plan. Keep this section brief — the goal is to demonstrate awareness, not to conduct a full risk analysis. Flag any risks that require executive decision-making to resolve.
