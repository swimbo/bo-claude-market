## Key Achievements

**Purpose**: Recognize and document the employee's most important accomplishments
**Target length**: ~200 words

Highlight 3-5 specific achievements from the review period. For each achievement:

- Describe what was accomplished with concrete details.
- Quantify the impact where possible (revenue generated, time saved, problems solved).
- Note any challenges overcome in the process.
- Connect the achievement to team or company goals.

Be specific and generous — this section should feel like genuine recognition, not a checkbox exercise. Include achievements that go beyond the job description: mentoring, cross-team collaboration, process improvements, or culture contributions. Reference any specific feedback received from peers, stakeholders, or customers.

## Goal Progress Assessment

**Purpose**: Evaluate progress against previously set goals objectively
**Target length**: ~200 words

Review each goal set at the beginning of the review period. For each goal:

- State the original goal.
- Assess the outcome: fully met, partially met, exceeded, or not met.
- Provide brief context — what factors contributed to the result?

If goals were not met, distinguish between factors within the employee's control and external circumstances. If goals shifted during the period due to changing priorities, acknowledge that the goalpost moved. This section should feel fair and balanced. End with an overall assessment of goal completion and what it indicates about the employee's performance trajectory.

## Core Competencies Evaluation

**Purpose**: Assess the employee's demonstrated skills and behaviors
**Target length**: ~200 words

Evaluate 4-6 core competencies relevant to the role. Common competencies include: technical skills, communication, collaboration, leadership, problem-solving, and adaptability. For each competency:

- Assess the current level: developing, proficient, strong, or exceptional.
- Provide one specific example that illustrates the assessment.
- Note any change from the previous review period (improved, stable, declined).

Be honest and evidence-based. Avoid the tendency to rate everything as "meets expectations" — differentiation helps the employee understand their true strengths and development areas. If the competency framework has changed since the last review, explain the new criteria.

## Areas for Development

**Purpose**: Identify growth opportunities with constructive, actionable guidance
**Target length**: ~200 words

Identify 2-3 areas where the employee can grow. For each area:

- Describe the gap between current performance and the desired level.
- Provide a specific example that illustrates the development need.
- Suggest concrete development actions: training, stretch assignments, mentoring, or practice.
- Explain why developing this area matters for the employee's career and the team.

Frame development areas as opportunities, not deficiencies. The tone should be supportive and forward-looking. Avoid surprises — development areas discussed in the review should align with feedback given throughout the period. If the employee is aware of and already working on these areas, acknowledge their efforts.

## Goals for Next Period

**Purpose**: Set clear, measurable goals that drive performance and growth
**Target length**: ~150 words

Collaboratively define 3-5 goals for the next review period. Each goal should follow the SMART framework:

- **Specific**: What exactly will be accomplished.
- **Measurable**: How success will be quantified.
- **Achievable**: Realistic given resources and timeline.
- **Relevant**: Connected to team or company objectives.
- **Time-bound**: When the goal should be completed.

Include a mix of performance goals (what to deliver) and development goals (how to grow). Ensure goals are challenging but achievable — stretch goals are motivating, impossible goals are demoralizing. Specify any resources or support the employee will need to achieve each goal.

## Career Development Discussion

**Purpose**: Explore the employee's career aspirations and create a development path
**Target length**: ~150 words

Document the employee's stated career aspirations and interests. Assess alignment between their aspirations and available opportunities within the organization. Identify 1-2 specific actions that will move them toward their career goals: skill development, visibility opportunities, rotational assignments, or mentorship connections. If there is a gap between aspirations and current readiness, discuss it honestly and outline the bridge. This section should feel like a genuine investment in the employee's future, not a formality.

## Action Items

**Purpose**: Capture specific follow-up commitments from both manager and employee
**Target length**: ~100 words

List 3-5 concrete action items with owners and deadlines. Include both manager commitments (providing resources, making introductions, removing blockers) and employee commitments (skill development, goal milestones, behavior changes). Specify the follow-up cadence — when will progress on these action items be reviewed? Both parties should leave the review with clear, documented next steps. This section serves as the accountability mechanism for the entire review.
