## Executive Summary

**Purpose**: Provide a standalone overview of the market analysis and its implications
**Target length**: ~200 words

Summarize the market analysis in a format that a busy executive can read in two minutes and take away the key insights. State the market size and growth trajectory. Identify the most significant opportunity and the most serious threat. Provide the headline strategic recommendation. Include 2-3 key data points that anchor the analysis. End with the decision or action this analysis is meant to inform. Write this section last, after completing the full analysis, to ensure it accurately reflects the findings.

## Market Overview

**Purpose**: Define the market landscape with data-driven context
**Target length**: ~300 words

Define the market segment being analyzed with precision — boundaries, inclusions, and exclusions. Present the total addressable market (TAM), serviceable addressable market (SAM), and serviceable obtainable market (SOM) if applicable.

Provide key market metrics:
- Current market size and historical growth.
- Projected growth rate and the drivers behind it.
- Market maturity stage (emerging, growing, mature, declining).
- Customer segments and their relative sizes.

Identify the macro forces shaping the market: technology shifts, regulatory changes, demographic trends, and economic conditions. Use data from credible sources and cite them. Include a market map or segmentation visual if it aids understanding. The reader should finish this section with a clear picture of the playing field.

## Competitive Analysis

**Purpose**: Map the competitive landscape and assess relative positioning
**Target length**: ~300 words

Profile 3-5 key competitors. For each:

- **Market position**: Market share, revenue, growth rate.
- **Strategy**: How they compete — price, differentiation, niche, or innovation.
- **Strengths**: What they do well and why customers choose them.
- **Weaknesses**: Where they fall short and where they are vulnerable.
- **Recent moves**: Product launches, partnerships, pricing changes, or market exits.

Identify the competitive dynamics: Is the market consolidating or fragmenting? Are new entrants disrupting incumbents? Is competition primarily on price, features, brand, or distribution? Assess your company's competitive position honestly — where do you lead and where do you lag? Include a competitive positioning matrix or comparison table for clarity.

## SWOT Analysis

**Purpose**: Synthesize internal and external factors into a strategic framework
**Target length**: ~300 words

Conduct a SWOT analysis that connects internal capabilities with external dynamics:

**Strengths**: Internal advantages that can be leveraged. Be specific — "strong brand" is less useful than "brand recognition among enterprise buyers with 72% aided awareness." Identify which strengths are durable and which are at risk of erosion.

**Weaknesses**: Internal limitations that must be addressed or mitigated. Be honest — the value of SWOT is in surfacing uncomfortable truths. Distinguish between weaknesses that can be fixed and those that are structural constraints.

**Opportunities**: External developments that the company can capitalize on. Prioritize by size, timing, and fit with company capabilities. Identify what is required to capture each opportunity.

**Threats**: External risks that could undermine the company's position. Assess likelihood and impact. Identify early warning signals for each threat. For each threat, note which strengths can be deployed as countermeasures.

Connect the quadrants: Which strengths can capture which opportunities? Which weaknesses expose you to which threats? This cross-referencing is where SWOT becomes strategic rather than descriptive.

## Market Opportunities

**Purpose**: Identify and prioritize the most promising growth opportunities
**Target length**: ~300 words

Present 3-5 market opportunities ranked by strategic attractiveness. For each opportunity:

- **Description**: What the opportunity is and why it exists.
- **Size**: The revenue potential or market value.
- **Timing**: Is this opportunity emerging, peaking, or closing?
- **Fit**: How well does it align with company capabilities and strategy?
- **Requirements**: What investments, capabilities, or partnerships are needed to capture it?
- **Competition**: Who else is pursuing this opportunity and what is their progress?

Use a prioritization framework (size vs. feasibility, attractiveness vs. fit) to rank opportunities. Identify any opportunities that are time-sensitive — first-mover advantages or closing windows. Recommend which opportunities to pursue now, which to plan for, and which to monitor.

## Risk Assessment

**Purpose**: Evaluate external threats and their potential impact on strategy
**Target length**: ~200 words

Assess 4-6 external risks facing the company in this market. For each risk:

- **Description**: What the risk is and how it could materialize.
- **Likelihood**: The probability of occurrence in the analysis timeframe.
- **Impact**: The potential effect on revenue, market position, or operations.
- **Velocity**: How quickly the risk could materialize.
- **Indicators**: Early warning signals to monitor.
- **Mitigation**: Recommended actions to reduce exposure.

Distinguish between risks that are within the company's control (competitive response, product quality) and those that are not (regulatory change, macroeconomic shifts). Identify any risks that are correlated — if one materializes, do others become more likely? Recommend a monitoring cadence for each risk.

## Strategic Recommendations

**Purpose**: Translate the analysis into clear, prioritized strategic actions
**Target length**: ~300 words

Present 3-5 strategic recommendations that synthesize the market overview, competitive analysis, SWOT, opportunities, and risks. For each recommendation:

- **Action**: State the recommended action clearly.
- **Rationale**: Connect it to specific findings from the analysis.
- **Priority**: Rank by importance and urgency.
- **Timeline**: When to begin and expected duration.
- **Expected outcome**: What success looks like.
- **Dependencies**: What must be in place for this recommendation to succeed.

Be specific and actionable — "expand into the mid-market segment" is more useful than "pursue growth." Address trade-offs between recommendations: if resources are constrained, which should be prioritized? Identify the one recommendation that would have the greatest impact if executed well. Close with a summary of the recommended strategic posture: attack, defend, invest, or wait.

## Investment Requirements

**Purpose**: Estimate the resources needed to execute the strategic recommendations
**Target length**: ~200 words

Provide a high-level estimate of the investment required to pursue the recommended strategy:

- **Financial**: Budget requirements by category (product, sales, marketing, infrastructure).
- **People**: Headcount additions, skill gaps to fill, organizational changes.
- **Technology**: Platform, tools, or infrastructure investments.
- **Partnerships**: Strategic relationships to develop or formalize.
- **Timeline**: Phased investment plan aligned with milestones.

Present the investment in the context of expected return — what is the projected ROI or payback period? Compare the cost of investing with the cost of inaction. If the investment is significant, suggest a phased approach with stage-gate decisions. Identify any investments that are required regardless of which strategic direction is chosen (no-regret moves).
