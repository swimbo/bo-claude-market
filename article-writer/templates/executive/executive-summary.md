## Situation Overview

**Purpose**: Provide context so the decision-maker understands the current state
**Target length**: ~150 words

State the situation concisely. What is happening, why does it require attention, and what triggered this analysis? Include only the most relevant background — executives have limited time and need to get oriented quickly. Reference any previous decisions, initiatives, or events that led to this moment. Quantify the situation where possible: market size, revenue at stake, number of affected customers or employees. End with a clear statement of the decision or action being requested.

## Key Findings

**Purpose**: Present the most important discoveries that inform the recommendation
**Target length**: ~200 words

Present 3-5 key findings in order of importance. Each finding should be a clear, declarative statement followed by one sentence of supporting evidence. Use data and specifics rather than generalizations. If findings come from research, analysis, or stakeholder interviews, briefly note the source for credibility. Highlight any findings that were unexpected or that challenge existing assumptions. These findings should logically lead to the recommendation that follows — the reader should be able to anticipate the recommendation after reading this section.

## Recommendation

**Purpose**: State the recommended course of action with clear rationale
**Target length**: ~200 words

State the recommendation directly in the opening sentence. Then explain the rationale: Why is this the best option? What alternatives were considered and why were they rejected? Reference the key findings that support this recommendation. Include the expected outcome — what will success look like? Address the financial implications: cost, ROI, budget source, and payback period. If the recommendation requires phased implementation, outline the phases briefly with key milestones and timeline.

## Risk Assessment

**Purpose**: Identify and address the key risks so the decision-maker can proceed informed
**Target length**: ~150 words

Present 3-4 key risks in a risk/mitigation format. For each risk:

- **Risk**: Describe the risk in one sentence.
- **Likelihood**: High, medium, or low.
- **Impact**: What happens if the risk materializes.
- **Mitigation**: The planned response to manage the risk.

Be honest about risks — executives distrust documents that present no downside. Include both implementation risks and market/external risks. Identify any risks that would require revisiting the recommendation if they materialize.

## Next Steps

**Purpose**: Define the immediate actions required to move forward
**Target length**: ~100 words

List 3-5 specific next steps with owners, deadlines, and expected outcomes. Identify the first decision needed from the executive: approval, budget allocation, resource assignment, or stakeholder communication. Include a proposed date for the next review or status update. End with a clear ask — what do you need from the reader right now?
