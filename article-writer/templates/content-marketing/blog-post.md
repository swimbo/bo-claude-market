## Introduction

**Purpose**: Hook the reader and establish the thesis of the post
**Target length**: ~150 words

Open with a compelling hook — a surprising statistic, a provocative question, or a relatable scenario that draws the reader in immediately. Follow the hook with 1-2 sentences of context that bridge to your thesis. End the introduction with a clear thesis statement that tells the reader exactly what they will learn or gain from reading this post. The introduction should create urgency and make the reader feel that continuing is worth their time.

## Main Content

**Purpose**: Deliver the three key points with supporting evidence and examples
**Target length**: ~650 words

Organize the body around three distinct key points, each in its own subsection with a descriptive subheading. For each point:

1. State the point clearly in 1-2 sentences.
2. Provide supporting evidence — data, research, expert quotes, or real-world examples.
3. Explain why this point matters to the reader and how they can act on it.

Use transitional sentences between points to maintain flow. Break up long paragraphs for readability. Include at least one concrete example or anecdote per point to make abstract ideas tangible. Where possible, anticipate and address reader objections.

## Conclusion

**Purpose**: Recap the main takeaways and drive the reader toward a specific action
**Target length**: ~200 words

Briefly summarize the three main points without simply repeating them — reframe them in terms of the reader's benefit. Then deliver a clear call-to-action: what should the reader do next? This could be signing up for a newsletter, trying a technique, sharing the post, leaving a comment, or exploring a related resource. End with a memorable closing line that reinforces the post's core message and leaves a lasting impression.
