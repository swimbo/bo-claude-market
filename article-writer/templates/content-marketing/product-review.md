## Overview

**Purpose**: Introduce the product and set expectations for the review
**Target length**: ~200 words

Open with a brief description of the product, its manufacturer, and the problem it aims to solve. Mention how long you have been using or testing the product to establish credibility. State the price range and where the product fits in the market (budget, mid-range, premium). Provide a one-sentence summary verdict to give readers who skim a quick takeaway. Set the stage for the detailed analysis that follows.

## Key Features

**Purpose**: Deep-dive into the most important features and how they perform
**Target length**: ~500 words

Break this section into 3-5 feature subsections. For each feature:

- Describe what the feature is and how it works.
- Share your hands-on experience using it.
- Compare it to similar features in competing products when relevant.
- Rate or assess the feature's quality, reliability, and value.

Be specific — use measurements, benchmarks, or concrete observations rather than vague adjectives. Include screenshots, specifications, or data where applicable. Address both power users and casual users.

## Pros and Cons

**Purpose**: Provide a balanced, honest assessment of strengths and weaknesses
**Target length**: ~400 words

List 4-6 pros and 3-5 cons. For each item, provide a brief explanation (1-2 sentences) rather than just a bullet point. Be honest about shortcomings — credibility comes from balanced reviews. Mention any deal-breakers or standout advantages that would significantly influence a buying decision. If a con has a workaround, mention it. Consider how the pros and cons shift depending on user type.

## Verdict

**Purpose**: Deliver the final recommendation with context for different buyer types
**Target length**: ~400 words

Synthesize the review into a clear recommendation. State who this product is best for and who should look elsewhere. Address value for money directly — is it worth the price? Compare briefly to 1-2 alternatives the reader might also be considering. Provide an overall rating or score if appropriate. End with a definitive recommendation: buy, skip, or wait, along with a link or direction for where to purchase.
