## The Main Point

**Purpose**: State your most important insight immediately and boldly
**Target length**: ~100 words

Do not bury the lead. Open with your core insight stated as clearly and directly as possible. This is the inverted pyramid approach — the most important information comes first. State it as a confident declaration, not a question. The reader should know within the first two sentences exactly what you believe and why it matters. If they stop reading here, they should still walk away with the key idea.

## Why This Is True

**Purpose**: Provide the evidence and reasoning that supports your main point
**Target length**: ~150 words

Back up your insight with 2-3 pieces of supporting evidence. Use data, research, expert opinions, or real-world examples. Each piece of evidence should directly reinforce the main point. Be concise — this is not a research paper. The goal is to build enough credibility that the reader trusts your insight. Address the most obvious counterargument briefly to show you have considered alternative perspectives.

## Context and Examples

**Purpose**: Enrich the insight with background and concrete illustrations
**Target length**: ~150 words

Provide context that helps the reader understand why this insight is relevant now. What trends, events, or shifts make this timely? Include 1-2 concrete examples that show the insight in action — real companies, real people, real situations. Examples transform abstract insights into tangible understanding. Choose examples the reader can relate to or learn from.

## What This Means for You

**Purpose**: Make the insight personal and actionable for the reader
**Target length**: ~100 words

Translate the insight into direct relevance for the reader. How does this change what they should do, think, or prioritize? Provide one specific action they can take based on this insight. Close with a forward-looking statement that leaves the reader feeling informed and empowered. Keep it concise — the pyramid narrows to a sharp, memorable point.
