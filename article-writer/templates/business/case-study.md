## Challenge

**Purpose**: Establish the problem context and make the reader empathize with the situation
**Target length**: ~400 words

Begin with a brief introduction to the client — their industry, size, and relevant background. Then describe the challenge they faced in specific, concrete terms. Avoid vague descriptions; use data points, timelines, and real consequences to illustrate the severity of the problem.

Address the business impact: What was the cost of inaction? How was this challenge affecting revenue, operations, customer satisfaction, or growth? Include a direct quote from the client describing the challenge in their own words if possible.

Explain what solutions the client had tried before and why they fell short. This builds the case for why a new approach was needed and sets up the solution section.

## Solution

**Purpose**: Detail the approach taken and why it was the right fit
**Target length**: ~600 words

Describe the solution in a logical sequence: discovery, strategy, implementation. For each phase:

- **Discovery**: How was the problem diagnosed? What research or analysis was conducted?
- **Strategy**: What approach was chosen and why? What alternatives were considered and rejected?
- **Implementation**: Walk through the key steps of execution, including timeline, team involvement, and any obstacles overcome during the process.

Be specific about the tools, methods, or frameworks used. Include enough technical detail to be credible without overwhelming non-expert readers. Use visuals, diagrams, or process flows if they add clarity. Include a client quote about the experience of working through the solution.

## Results

**Purpose**: Showcase measurable outcomes that prove the solution worked
**Target length**: ~500 words

Lead with the most impressive metric or outcome. Present results in concrete, quantifiable terms:

- Revenue impact (percentage increase, dollar amount)
- Efficiency gains (time saved, cost reduced)
- Customer impact (satisfaction scores, retention rates)
- Scale achieved (users, transactions, market expansion)

Use before-and-after comparisons to make the impact vivid. Include a timeline — how quickly were results achieved? Present results in multiple formats: narrative, bullet points, and a data highlight box. Include a client testimonial that speaks to the impact in their own words.

## Key Takeaways

**Purpose**: Distill transferable lessons for the reader's own context
**Target length**: ~500 words

Extract 3-5 key lessons from the case study that readers can apply to their own situations. For each takeaway:

- State the lesson clearly.
- Explain why it matters beyond this specific case.
- Provide a practical suggestion for how the reader can apply it.

End with a broader industry insight or trend that this case study illustrates. Include a call-to-action inviting readers to discuss their own challenges or explore similar solutions.
