## The Prediction

**Purpose**: State the prediction boldly and specifically so it can be evaluated
**Target length**: ~150 words

Open with your prediction stated clearly and specifically. Avoid vague predictions ("AI will change everything") in favor of specific, falsifiable ones ("Within 3 years, 50% of customer service interactions will be handled entirely by AI without human escalation"). Include a timeframe — predictions without deadlines are just opinions. Briefly explain why this prediction matters and who it will affect most. Acknowledge your confidence level honestly: Is this a high-conviction bet or an educated guess? Specificity and intellectual honesty separate credible predictions from clickbait.

## Why I Believe This Will Happen

**Purpose**: Present the evidence and reasoning behind the prediction
**Target length**: ~250 words

Build your case with 3-4 pieces of supporting evidence:

- **Current trends**: What existing data or trajectories point toward this outcome?
- **Leading indicators**: What early signals suggest this is already beginning?
- **Analogies**: Have similar patterns played out in other industries or time periods?
- **Expert alignment**: Do credible voices in the field see similar signals?

For each piece of evidence, explain the connection to your prediction explicitly. Address the strongest counterargument: What could prevent this prediction from coming true? What assumptions are you making that could be wrong? This balanced analysis builds credibility — readers trust predictors who acknowledge uncertainty over those who project false certainty.

## What It Means

**Purpose**: Explore the implications and second-order effects of the prediction
**Target length**: ~200 words

If this prediction comes true, what changes? Map out the implications across multiple dimensions: industry structure, job roles, consumer behavior, competitive dynamics, or regulatory landscape. Identify winners and losers — who benefits and who is disrupted? Explore second-order effects: the consequences of the consequences that most people do not think about. This section should make the reader see the future differently, even if they are not fully convinced by the prediction itself. The analysis should be more valuable than the prediction.

## How to Prepare

**Purpose**: Give the reader actionable steps to position themselves for this future
**Target length**: ~200 words

Regardless of whether the reader fully agrees with the prediction, offer practical preparation steps. Frame them as "no-regret moves" — actions that are valuable whether or not the prediction comes true. Suggest 3-4 specific actions:

- Skills to develop or invest in.
- Relationships or networks to build.
- Experiments to run or projects to start.
- Risks to hedge or positions to take.

Close with a time-sensitive element: What should the reader do in the next 30 days? The best prediction posts do not just inform — they mobilize.
