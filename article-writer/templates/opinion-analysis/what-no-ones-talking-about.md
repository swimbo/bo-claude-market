## The Overlooked Issue

**Purpose**: Name the issue clearly and show that it is being neglected
**Target length**: ~250 words

State the issue directly — what is the thing that no one is talking about? Provide enough context that the reader can understand the issue even if they have never considered it before. Illustrate the neglect: How much attention is this getting compared to what it deserves? Use a contrast — point to the topics that dominate conversation and show what is being crowded out. Include a specific example or data point that makes the issue concrete. The reader should feel a growing sense of "wait, why have I not heard about this?"

## Why It's Being Ignored

**Purpose**: Diagnose the structural, psychological, or cultural reasons for the blind spot
**Target length**: ~250 words

Explore why this issue is flying under the radar. Common explanations include:

- **Complexity**: It is too nuanced for headlines or social media.
- **Inconvenience**: Acknowledging it would require uncomfortable action.
- **Misaligned incentives**: The people in positions to raise it benefit from the status quo.
- **Distraction**: More dramatic or attention-grabbing issues are absorbing all the oxygen.
- **Gradual onset**: The change is slow enough to go unnoticed day-to-day.

Be specific about which of these apply to your issue and why. This diagnostic section demonstrates thought leadership — you are not just pointing out a problem but explaining the systemic failure that allows it to persist.

## Why This Matters

**Purpose**: Build urgency by connecting the issue to concrete consequences
**Target length**: ~200 words

Make the case for why this issue deserves attention now. What are the consequences of continued neglect? Who is being harmed, and how will the harm compound over time? Use data, projections, or expert warnings to quantify the stakes. If the issue has a tipping point or point of no return, identify when it arrives. Connect the issue to something the reader already cares about — their industry, their community, their values. The goal is to transform indifference into concern.

## Time to Pay Attention

**Purpose**: Mobilize the reader to engage with the issue and spread awareness
**Target length**: ~200 words

Now that the reader is aware and concerned, what should they do? Offer 2-3 specific actions: educate themselves further (provide resources), advocate within their sphere of influence, change a specific behavior, or support an organization working on the issue. Make the actions accessible — the reader should feel that they can make a difference, not that the problem is too large. Close with a forward-looking statement about what becomes possible when people start paying attention. Invite the reader to share this piece and start conversations — awareness is the first step toward change.
