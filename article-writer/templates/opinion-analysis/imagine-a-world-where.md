## The Vision

**Purpose**: Paint a vivid, compelling picture of the possible future
**Target length**: ~250 words

Open with "Imagine a world where..." and bring it to life with specific, sensory details. Do not describe the future in abstract terms — show what a day looks like in this world. What does a person experience? What problems have been solved? What new possibilities have opened up? Use concrete scenarios rather than statistics. The reader should be able to close their eyes and picture this future.

Balance aspiration with plausibility. The best visions are ambitious enough to be exciting but grounded enough to feel achievable. Avoid utopian fantasies — include enough texture and nuance that the vision feels like a real future, not a fairy tale. Hint at the challenges that remain even in this better world.

## What Makes This Possible

**Purpose**: Ground the vision in real-world trends and emerging capabilities
**Target length**: ~250 words

Connect the vision to current reality by identifying 3-4 enabling trends, technologies, or shifts that are already underway. For each:

- Describe the trend and its current trajectory.
- Explain how it contributes to making the vision achievable.
- Cite evidence — data, examples, early adopters — that the trend is real and accelerating.

Show the reader that this is not science fiction but an extrapolation of things already happening. If the vision requires breakthroughs that have not yet occurred, be honest about that and explain why you believe they are within reach. This section transforms the vision from a dream into a plausible forecast.

## Why This Matters

**Purpose**: Establish urgency and stakes — what we gain and what we lose
**Target length**: ~200 words

Make the case for why this vision is worth pursuing. What are the costs of failing to achieve it? Who benefits if we succeed, and who suffers if we do not? Connect the vision to values the reader holds: equity, progress, sustainability, human dignity, or prosperity. Address the skeptic: Why is this not just nice-to-have wishful thinking? What concrete problems does this vision solve? Create a sense of urgency — this is not a distant possibility but something we need to start working toward now.

## How We Build It

**Purpose**: Chart a practical path from the present to the envisioned future
**Target length**: ~200 words

Transition from inspiration to action. Outline 3-4 concrete steps that individuals, organizations, or communities can take to move toward this vision. Prioritize actions that are achievable today with existing resources. Identify who needs to lead each step and what success looks like in the near term. Acknowledge that the full vision may take years or decades, but meaningful progress can start immediately.

Close with an invitation for the reader to join the effort. Whether it is a mindset shift, a project to start, a community to join, or a conversation to have — give the reader a first step they can take before the day is over. End on a note of grounded optimism.
