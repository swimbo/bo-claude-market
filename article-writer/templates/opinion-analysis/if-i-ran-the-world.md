## What's Wrong Today

**Purpose**: Critique the current state with specificity and credibility
**Target length**: ~300 words

Identify 3-4 specific problems with how the entity or system currently operates. For each problem:

- Describe what is happening with concrete examples.
- Explain who is negatively affected and how.
- Identify the root cause — not just the symptom, but why it persists.

Balance criticism with fairness. Acknowledge any genuine constraints or trade-offs that created the current situation. Avoid straw-manning — critique the strongest version of the status quo, not the weakest. Your credibility as a visionary depends on your accuracy as a diagnostician. The reader should finish this section thinking, "They really understand the problem."

## My Vision

**Purpose**: Present a compelling, specific alternative for how things should work
**Target length**: ~400 words

Lay out your vision with enough detail that the reader can picture it. For each change you would make:

- Describe the new approach clearly.
- Explain the mechanism — how and why it would work.
- Address the transition — how you would get from here to there.
- Anticipate the objection — what critics would say and why they are wrong (or partially right).

Be bold but not reckless. The best visions are ambitious enough to inspire but grounded enough to be credible. Include examples or precedents where elements of your vision have already been tested or proven elsewhere. If your vision requires trade-offs, name them honestly. Show the reader you have thought this through beyond the appealing surface.

## Why This Matters

**Purpose**: Connect your vision to broader stakes and inspire action
**Target length**: ~300 words

Zoom out from the specifics to explain what is at stake. What becomes possible if your vision is implemented? What is lost if the status quo continues? Connect your vision to larger trends, values, or movements that the reader cares about. Address the "so what" for different audiences: practitioners, leaders, customers, society.

Close with a call to action. You may not actually run the world (or the company), but you can influence it. Invite the reader to adopt elements of your vision in their own sphere. Ask them to share, discuss, and debate. The goal is not to win agreement but to shift the conversation — to make people question what they previously accepted as given.
